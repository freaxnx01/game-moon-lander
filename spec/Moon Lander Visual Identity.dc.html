S-01 DIRECTION & RATIONALE
The Moon at local dawn: sunlight raking in at 2°, every rising slope a hard white edge, every valley in kilometres of cast shadow. Where 1a draws the world as a diagram, this direction photographs it — light is the terrain data. You judge slope by which edges burn and which vanish. The craft is dark hardware, read by its sun-side rim, a warm cabin window and one retro-reflective decal. The signal trio (teal / amber / red) is shared with 1a and remains the only judgment color on screen — everything else is monochrome. No ambient fill, no glow, no panel chrome: the HUD is bare type floating on void.
01 — LIGHT IS TERRAIN DATA
02 — COLOR = CONSEQUENCE
03 — DARKNESS IS ALLOWED
NOT THIS: blue-gray ambient mush, bloom/glow, panel boxes, survey grids — the drafting layer belongs to 1a.

S-01 DIRECTION & RATIONALE
The game renders as a live page from a lunar-module flight manual: ink line-work on a drafting field, survey grid, dimension callouts. The fantasy is precision, not spectacle — you are flying the diagram, and the HUD grades every second of the burn. Color is rationed: the world holds four inks; teal, amber and red are reserved exclusively for judgment (safe / caution / exceed), so the eye learns that color = consequence. No CRT glow, no neon bloom, no scanlines — the tension of a descent is a steady needle, not a lens flare.
01 — FLY THE DIAGRAM
02 — COLOR = CONSEQUENCE
03 — PRINT, NOT PHOSPHOR
NOT THIS: neon wireframe arcade, CRT bloom, parallax haze, particle spam.

S-04 TERRAIN & PADS
— Profile: 2 px ink contour over Regolith fill (darkest value on screen) — terrain always separates from sky, even at horizon.

— Pads carry the only environmental color. Teal chevrons point to centerline; beacon dots mark usable width; gold + pennant + ×3 numeral = bonus (never gold alone).
— Hazards annotated in amber survey tags. Slopes > 8° never draw a pad strip, so “landable” has exactly one look.
